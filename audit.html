Competitive Audit

  Learning Apps
Question Kahoot Quizizz Yousician
Engagement: How engaging is the app? Is it visually appealing? Does it have sound and animation to capture the user's attention? Does it have a good balance of challenge and reward? Kahoot is an amazingly engaging game where students or friends have to quickly answer questions and who ever performs the best takes first place on the leaderboard. The music changes with each new question asked creating a feeling of tension and when the question is finished it goes to a relaxing fade out which heavily grabs the users attention. The challenge depends on the category of questions being asked and the reward you get is being above all your other mates at the top of the pedistal. (7/10) Quizizz is based off of the already popular game kahoot. Even though this game is based on kahoot, it improves heavily in engagement. This app has multiple game modes with a live tracker for where you are in the leaderboard, there are no pauses, there is no music change it is just you, versus your class mates, you have to keep answering questions before the time runs out and whoever collects the most coins or goes the farthest (depending on the game mode) wins at the end. It is extremely visually appealing with very fluid animations and powerups. There is a very good balance of challenge and reward and a lot of luck is also present. (9/10) Yousician is a music learning app with offers engaging videos and animations to help the user learn how to play a musical instrument of their choice, it is very visually appealing with smooth fluid animations for when you get a note correct and when you make a mistake, there is music which plays in the menu and then a song plays which you will learn before the lesson starts. It offers a very good balance between challenge and reward and the challenge increases further if you pick a higher difficulty level. (8/10)
Progress tracking: How well does the app track the user's progress? Does it provide feedback and recommendations to help users improve their performance? Does it use data to personalize the learning experience? The app doesn’t track any personal progress, although if you are part of a class the teacher can see your live quiz progress it doesn’t submit any feedback or recommendations to help the user improve, and uses no data to personalize the experience. (2/10) The app doesn’t track what quizzes you complete, but there are rewards for how many quizzes you do and how many first places you get, those rewards consist of rare profile pictures. The app however doesn’t provide any feedback or recommendations, and doesn’t use data to personalize the user experience. (5/10) The app tracks your complete and in depth progress telling you where you went wrong, where you need to improve and what you need to completely re-do, it tracks your progress for what you have learned and gives you badges as you progress, it uses the data to personalize the experience and allows users to learn their favourite songs. (8/10)
User experience: How user-friendly is the app? Is it easy to navigate and use? Does it have clear instructions and prompts? Does it have a well-designed interface? The app is extremely user friendly with easy navigation to find new quizzes and to select an answer to a question. It is extremely well designed and has very good prompts. (9/10) The app is also extremely user-friendly, it allows for a nice smooth interaction with clearly labelled prompts and instructions to help the user navigate through it’s very simple and easy to understand interface. (9/10) This app provides an extremely nice and user-friendly experience it is very easy to navigate and to find lessons you haven’t done yet, it also provides the user with well layed out prompts to help them navigate the app. It has an extremely nice designed user interface. (9/10)
